COMBINED DEFENCE SERVICES

CDS is a competitive examination in India conducted by the Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) to recruit officers for the Indian Armed Forces.

Key features of CDS:

Eligibility: Candidates who have completed their bachelor's degree can apply. Different academies (Indian Military Academy, Indian Naval Academy, Air Force Academy, and Officers' Training Academy) have slightly different eligibility criteria.

Examination Pattern: The CDS examination consists of a written test and an interview.

Written Test: It includes English, General Knowledge, and Elementary Mathematics (for certain posts). The questions are objective type.

Interview/Personality Test: Successful candidates in the written exam are called for an interview by the Services Selection Board (SSB).

Selection for Different Academies: Candidates can join different academies like the Indian Military Academy, Indian Naval Academy, Air Force Academy, and Officers' Training Academy based on their preferences and performance.

Training and Commission: Upon successful completion of training, candidates are commissioned as officers in the Indian Army, Navy, or Air Force.

The CDS examination is a highly competitive process and provides an opportunity for individuals to join the defense services of India as officers.
