AIR FORCE COMMON ADMISSION TEST

AFCAT, which stands for Air Force Common Admission Test, is an entrance exam conducted by the Indian Air Force (IAF) for recruiting officers in the flying, technical, and ground duty branches. The exam is held twice a year, usually in February and August, and is open to both male and female candidates who aspire to join the Indian Air Force as officers.

Here are some key points about AFCAT:

Branches and Courses:

1. Flying Branch: Pilots for fixed-wing aircraft.
2. Technical Branch: Engineers for various technical roles.
3. Ground Duty Branch: Officers in administrative, logistics, accounts, education, and meteorology.

Eligibility Criteria:

1. Educational Qualifications: The requirements vary for different branches. A candidate should have completed a graduation degree in a relevant field.
2. Age Limit: The age limit varies for different branches, but generally, it is between 20 and 24 years for the Flying Branch and 20 to 26 years for the Technical and Ground Duty Branches.

AFCAT Exam Pattern:

AFCAT is a computer-based test.
The exam consists of multiple-choice questions (MCQs).
The duration of the exam is 2 hours, and it includes questions on General Awareness, Verbal Ability in English, Numerical Ability, and Reasoning and Military Aptitude Test.
Candidates applying for the Flying Branch are required to appear for additional EKT (Engineering Knowledge Test) for technical branches.

Selection Process:

The selection process includes the AFCAT written exam, followed by Air Force Selection Board (AFSB) testing.
The AFSB testing involves various stages such as Officer Intelligence Rating Test, Psychological Tests, Group Tests, and Personal Interview.
Medical Examination is also an integral part of the selection process.

Application Process:

The application process for AFCAT is conducted online.
Candidates need to register on the official website, fill in the application form, and pay the requisite fee.

Training:

Once selected, candidates undergo training at the Air Force Academy.

Service Commitment:

Candidates who successfully complete the training are commissioned as officers and have a mandatory service commitment with the Indian Air Force.

Preparation:

Aspirants can prepare for AFCAT by studying subjects like General Knowledge, English, Mathematics, and Reasoning.
Practice with previous years' question papers and take mock tests for better preparation.

Official Website:

For the most accurate and updated information, candidates should refer to the official Indian Air Force website https://careerindianairforce.cdac.in/
AFCAT is a highly competitive exam, and aspirants should prepare thoroughly to maximize their chances of success.
Additionally, candidates should stay updated with the latest notifications and changes in the exam pattern or eligibility criteria.
